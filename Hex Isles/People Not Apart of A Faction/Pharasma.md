The Pathfinder Goddess of the [[Soul]], life (birth and death), and prophecy. Information copied from the pathfinder wiki pages.

### Basic Description
**Pharasma**, **the Lady of Graves**, is the [goddess](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Goddess "Goddess") of birth, [death](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Death "Death"), fate, [prophecy](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Prophecy "Prophecy"), and [time](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Time "Time"). Gazing across all of time, she judges and shepherds [Golarion's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Golarion "Golarion") recently departed [souls](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Soul "Soul") to their final fates.[2](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-magic-second-edition-38--2) Upon death, souls migrate via the [River of Souls](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/River_of_Souls "River of Souls") to Pharasma's [Boneyard](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Boneyard "Boneyard") for her judgment atop an impossibly tall [spire](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma%27s_Spire "Pharasma's Spire") in the [Outer Sphere](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Outer_Sphere "Outer Sphere") that pierces the [Astral Plane](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Astral_Plane "Astral Plane").[3](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-and-magic-pathfinder-chronicles-30-31--3)


### Dogma
Pharasma makes no decision on whether a death is just or not; she views all with a cold and uncaring attitude, and decides on which of the Outer Planes a soul will spend eternity. Pharasma is also the goddess of birth and prophecy: from the moment a creature is born, she sees what its ultimate fate will be, but reserves final judgement until that soul finally stands before her.[3](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-and-magic-pathfinder-chronicles-30-31--3)[2](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-magic-second-edition-38--2)

While capable of withholding judgment on a soul, she has never done so, and willfully sends judged souls to their proper destinations even when they benefit deities she personally despises.[2](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-magic-second-edition-38--2)

As the goddess of death and rebirth, she abhors the [undead](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") and considers them a disruptive perversion of the natural cycle of souls.[2](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-magic-second-edition-38--2)[3](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-and-magic-pathfinder-chronicles-30-31--3)[4](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-4) She commands her followers and servants to destroy all undead beings.[2](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma#cite_note-gods-magic-second-edition-38--2)
